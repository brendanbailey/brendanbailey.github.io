Portfolio


Helping Diagnose Cervical Cancer with Neural Networks

A Kaggle Competiton on classifiying cervix types to aid the process of treating cervical cancer. We were given over a 1,000 cervix photos, and had to train a model on how to properly identify cervix type based on those photos. This competition is important because in areas where they do not have resources to do a pap smear, practitioners need to do a visual inspection, which is dependent on cervix type. This project is an exploration of convolutional neural networks, but ultimately a PCA SVM combo was used for the final submission.
Lightning Talk: Clustering with HDBSCAN

HDBSCAN has the benefits of being able to cluster varying shapes like DBSCAN. However, HDBSCAN also performs better on datasets of varying density and is faster with larger datasets. Click the link above to see my presentation on the benefits, how to implement, and how the HDBSCAN algorithm works.

Garnet Auto Check In with Selenium

Garnet is General Assembly's attendance system that students need to sign into everyday at 9am. You can only qualify for certain GA benefits if you have a limited number of tardies. This policy makes sense, but there is nothing worse than coming in before 9am and forgetting to sign in. This Selenium script was developed to help prevent students from getting tardies by automatically signing them into Garnet.
Which GA Campus is Most Happening?

One of the most valuable services GA provides are events. Events provide opportunities for networking enabling you to find your next job, team member, investor, or to just keep in the know. I webscraped the General Assembly website, so that I could compare which of the campuses had the most events.
General Assembly Breast Cancer Project

A General Assembly project where I was given a dataset of breast tumors and had to build a model, which would determine whether the tumor was benign or malignant based on its physical characteristics. The requirement was to use two different models and compare the performance. I used a logistic model and a random forest, and in the end went with the logistic due to its simplicity and ability to make inferences from it.
